📚 Learn About Velocity

What is Velocity?

Velocity is a vector quantity that describes both the speed and direction of an object's motion. Unlike speed, which is scalar, velocity changes when either magnitude or direction changes.

Projectile Motion

When an object is launched at an angle, it follows a curved path called a parabola. The motion can be separated into horizontal (constant) and vertical (accelerated) components.

Horizontal Component

The horizontal velocity remains constant throughout the flight (ignoring air resistance) because there's no horizontal force acting on the object.

Vertical Component

The vertical velocity changes due to gravity's acceleration. It decreases on the way up, becomes zero at maximum height, and increases (downward) on the way down.

Launch Angle Effect

A 45° angle provides the maximum range on level ground. Angles less than 45° travel less distance but land sooner. Angles greater than 45° reach higher but travel less horizontal distance.

Gravity's Role

Different celestial bodies have different gravitational accelerations. Try Earth (9.8 m/s²), Moon (1.62 m/s²), or Mars (3.7 m/s²) to see how it affects the trajectory!
